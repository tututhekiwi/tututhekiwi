He Wā Whakatā — A Moment to Reflect

Sometimes Tutu stops, listens to the wind through the harakeke, and breathes. This page is that same pause — a space for gratitude, presence, and connection to te ao hurihuri.

Karakia Timatanga

Whakataka te hau ki te uru,

Whakataka te hau ki te tonga,

Kia mākinakina ki uta,

Kia mātaratara ki tai,

E hī ake ana te atākura

He tio, he huka, he hau hū,

Tīhei mauri ora!

Reflect

Each feather we carry — each act of kindness, story shared, or laugh given — returns to the wind. Pause here. Breathe. Remember that everything alive is connected.

“God is larger than justice,” you said. And in this place, justice becomes compassion; structure becomes song; and Tutu — tiny, bright, joyful — reminds us that creation itself is play.
